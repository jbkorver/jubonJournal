Letters From Ralph

Ralph J. Mouw, a native of Orange City, Iowa, never forgot his roots – even when he was halfway across the world during World War II.

In this collection of letters, you’ll find a firsthand account of what life was like during the war at home and overseas. Although letters were heavily censored during the war years, the editors have added comments to place them in context.

RAGBRAI
By Owner: Bonnie Meier

On July 22 thousands of people will be gathering in Orange City to start the great bicycle ride across Iowa the next morning. They just announced this the evening of January 21 in Des Moines, Iowa. RAGBRAI stands for the Register’s Annual Great Bicycle Ride Across Iowa. This is a ride across Iowa that a couple of people who worked for the Des Moines Register started 45 years ago. It usually brings people from all over the world and they ride from the west end of the state to the east end in a week. RAGBRAI has gone through Orange City before, but it has never started here before. I’ll try to add more information in our next newsletter. This is actually pretty big news for our little town. According to news, this will be one of the easiest routes they have ever taken in terms of miles and of feet climb. Participants will peddle 411 miles and have 13, 078 feet of climb for the third easiest totals in RAGBRAI history.

Christmas Party Celebrated in January
By Owner: Bonnie Meier

On January 21 all those associated with Jubon Juweelen celebrated their Christmas party in the newly remodeled restaurant that used to be the Old Hatchery. It is now called the Orange City Downtown Grille and it is a beautiful place. The kitchen has been completely redone and the building has been extended 60 feet to the north. We had dinner in a private room with a lovely fireplace. Two brothers from New York are the chefs and everything was wonderful. It is actually not officially open yet, but Tonya Johnson, the manager said they needed a few parties on which to practice. I’m thinking of reserving a spot for next year’s Christmas party soon, because I’m sure once people eat there, reservations will fill up fast.
