Letters From Ralph

Ralph J. Mouw, a native of Orange City, Iowa, never forgot his roots - even when he was halfway across the world during World War II.

In this collection of letters, you'll find a firsthand account of what life was like during the war at home and overseas. Although letters were heavily censored during the war years, the editors have added comments to place them in context.

Farmers' Market
By Owner: Bonnie Meier

We are playing with the idea of taking part in the Saturday Farmers’ Market next year. Our Chamber is always looking at ways of making our city more vibrant and one of their newest ideas is to block off our main street on Saturdays and putting up tents for a Farmers’ Market. While most people think of Farmers’ Markets being mainly food, our Chamber Director, has been encouraging more of the regular businesses to take part as well. We could bring some of our discs, wooden shoes, books, etc. and it would also give us an opportunity to tell people about our new location. I thought it might also be a good idea to make some of the items in our new Dutch Cookbook and give away small samples and tell people on which page to find the recipe. – These are all thoughts for the future, but I think it is worth thinking about.


Juliana's Frisbee Competition Update
By Owner: Bonnie Meier

Recently, my daughter, Juliana Korver travelled to Berlin, Germany for the European Freestyle Open (EFO). She placed 3rd in Women's pairs and 4th in Mixed pairs. She was somewhat dissappointed in this outcome as she and her respective partners placed first in both Women's pairs and mixed pairs the previous day in the semi-final round, but did not play as cleanly in the finals. However, the EFO was simply a warmup for the Freestyle World Championships - the reason for the trip.

The World Championships were held in Trnava, Slovakia in conjunction with the Trnava Frisbee Games. It was a 4 day event. Juliana made it to the finals in both Women's pairs and mixed pairs. She and her partner, Ilka Simon, from Colgne, Germany ended up in second place in women's pairs. In mixed pairs, Juliana played with fellow San Diegian, Dave Schiller. They finished in 3rd place in the very difficult mixed pairs division. Juliana was extremely happy with these results as her goal for this year was to make the podium (top 3) which she did twice. She is already looking forward to next year's World Championships that will be held in Seattle, WA.
