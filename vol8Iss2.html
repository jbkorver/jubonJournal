Letters From Ralph

In this collection of letters, you’ll find a firsthand account of what life was like during the war at home and overseas. Although letters were heavily censored during the war years, the editors have added comments to place them in context.

Picture Sale
By Owner: Bonnie Meier

Our next promotion will start February 29 and run through March 5. It will feature all of the pictures in our store. We have a variety of prices available from very inexpensive to several originals, both oils and watercolors. All pictures will be 15% off their posted price. This is a first for us and may not happen again for a long time, if ever.


A very nice original that we have for sale is entitled "At the Hearth" by Freerk (Fred) Reinders. Fred is a Dutch artist who immigrated to the United States in 1893 when he was 19 years old. This picture is oil on Masonite and was created in 1956, three years before his death. The picture is 28" wide by 20" tall. His pictures rarely come up for sale or auction by private parties. Most of his notable paintings are owned by the Sioux County Courthouse and can be seen and are on permanent display in the main floor lobby. They have been on the main floor since 1914 and on the lobby’s west wall since 1920.


Other notable pieces include some original oils by Harriet Nordby. Harriet was born Harriet Schoep in Sioux County in 1933. She died in 2010 at the age of 76 in Brainard, Minnesota. She painted in a style call Contemporary Realism. She also painted under the name Sabrina Grey. Our largest picture by Harriet is a 22" x 30" oil in a beautiful frame depicting two young ladies with a piano and sells for $1500. Another oil is approximately 24" x 20" in a very ornate frame in wines and whites with a lot of texture and sells for $600. We also have a couple of prints selling for much less.


We have some watercolor tulips by Molly Van Roekel, a recent graduate of painting from the Memphis College of Art and they sell for $150. We have some beautiful works by Judy Thompson, but more about her later. We have many, many prints, mostly with Dutch scenes, that sell from just a few dollars to around $30 - $50. We also have some unique pieces of lace framed. If you can't come in, give us a call, and we'll send you pictures via e-mail. We also send UPS.
