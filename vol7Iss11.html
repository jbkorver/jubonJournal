Developing Dynamic Leaders (DDL) Class to be Offered
By Co-Owner: Bonnie Meier

Iowa State Extension will be offering a DDL class soon. We are in the planning stages now. DDL is a wonderful class that helps develop community leaders. I have taken the class twice myself and highly recommend it. The class will be offered in Sioux County and will be open to 30 – 40 participants. There are several positives about the class. One is that it helps develop a networking process among the upcoming leaders in the county. Two is that people will develop confidence in their own abilities to run for city councils, Extension boards, and other leadership positions. Three is that it puts one in touch with some of the many resources available through Iowa State University. It is amazing what is available to us, but many people do not realize it. There are many more positives. The class usually consists of around six units that are about three hours each. It often includes a meal, however, since we are still in the planning phase, those details are unavailable. The marvelous news is that we have a community leader from each of the Sioux County towns who will do the original planning. I had the privilege of putting the planning team together and everyone I asked said yes. I was overwhelmed by the positive response. We have people representing business, education, the faith community, finance, Ag, and many more areas. I helped organize an opportunity similar to this when I was a school superintendent in southwest Iowa and shortly after leaving that area, I had a man barge past my secretary into my office. My secretary was quite upset, but when I saw it was one of my classmates, I told her not to be alarmed. The tall farmer said that he had something for me and then handed me a doorknob. I asked him why and he replied, “That is for all the doors you opened for us in southwest Iowa.” That was over 20 years ago. I still have the doorknob and it still represents to me all the opportunities that Iowa State Extension can bring into every corner of our state.
