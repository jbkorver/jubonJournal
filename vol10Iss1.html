Letters From Ralph

Ralph J. Mouw, a native of Orange City, Iowa, never forgot his roots - even when he was halfway across the world during World War II.

In this collection of letters, you'gll find a firsthand account of what life was like during the war at home and overseas. Although letters were heavily censored during the war years, the editors have added comments to place them in context.

Perusing our Guest Book
By Owner: Bonnie Meier

I don't have any information right now because we are on vacation in San Diego. When I look to my right I see the white caps on the Pacific Ocean. When I look straight ahead I see the channel that boats, etc. use to get from Mission Bay to the Ocean. Yesterday we saw three porpoises swimming along the channel. We are in the last house on the point of Mission Beach with water on three sides of us. I understand it is icy in Orange City today so this is a nice reprieve. What makes it super special is that both our son and his family and daughter and her friend are here, too. In fact, Juliana just lives about three miles from where we are staying. When we get back home it will be almost March and spring can't be far behind. Yesterday we had the privilege of watching Juliana, her friend Rick, and multiple Freestyle World Champion, Dave Murphy freestyling on the beach at La Jolla Shores. Dave is a legend in the sport and there is no wondering why when you have the privledge of seeing him play. It was like watching something that you usually have to pay to see. Fun.
