Orange City's Tulip Festival

By Co-Founder: Bonnie Meier

Â Orange City will celebrate its 71st Tulip Festival the third weekend in May. This is a wonderful celebration of the community's Dutch Heritage. Activities include a fly-in breakfast and a 5K/10K Road Race. Exhibits featuring molen (dutch word for wind mills), tulip gardens and children's art will be available. Horse-drawn trolley rides begin at 9:00 am each day. The trollies are name after two former Queens of the Netherlands, Wilhelmina and Juliana. Other attractions include the Century Home, a flower show, the Sioux County Heritage Center, a quilt show, De Straatwinkel, Pedi-cabs, an antique tractor show, the Dutch Street Organ and on Saturday Art burst and Authors' Corner.

A time honored tradition for the Tulip Festival is the street inspection carried out by the Mayor and City Council. Traditionally, after inspection, they declare that the street is not clean enough for the parade to begin. This is the signal for many people young and old dressed in Dutch costumes to take to the streets with water and brooms to scrub it clean. Once the street is prepared the parade can begin and hosts many bands including MOC-Floyd Valley, which marches in wooden shoes. There are also Dutch Dancers, a puppet show, carnival rides, and a play. This year's performance is "Godspell". Â 

Come and join in the fun and help Orange City celebrate its heritage at the Tulip Festival May 19, 20, and 21. For more information visit http://www.octulipfestival.com/.
